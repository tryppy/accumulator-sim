About Accumulators

An accumulator is a structured product that allows investors to accumulate shares at a discounted price under certain market conditions.

How Accumulators Work

An accumulator allows investors to buy a fixed quantity of shares at a predetermined strike price (usually at a discount to the current market price) on a regular basis over a specified period.

  • Fixed quantity of shares accumulated daily/weekly
  • Predetermined strike price below market price
  • Knock-out feature if share price rises above certain level
  • Potential gearing/leverage features

Key Risk Factors

  • Market Risk: Obligation to buy even if share price falls significantly
  • Gearing Risk: Leveraged exposure can magnify losses
  • Opportunity Cost: Limited upside due to knock-out feature
  • Liquidity Risk: Usually not possible to terminate early
